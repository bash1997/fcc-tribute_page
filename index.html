Cicely Tyson

An embodiment of the American Dream

Cicely Tyson posing during a photoshoot styled by Samira Nasr.

Here are highlights of the life of the late great, Cicely Tyson

  • 1924 Ms. Tyson was born on December 19, to two immigrant parents

    • Ms. Tyson began her career as a fashion model, having been discovered by an Ebony magazine photographer.

  • 1951 Ms. Tyson's first action role was on a series called: Frontiers of Faith
  • Early 1960 Ms. Tyson was one the orginal cast members of Jean Genet's The Blacks. She starred along side Maya Angelou, James Earl Jones, Godfrey Cambridge, Louis Gossett Jr., and Charles Gordone.

    • Ms. Tyson was the first African American woman to wear her natural hair on a TV series.

  • 1963-1964 Ms. Tyson was cast member of a TV series and at the time, Ms. Tyson was the only person of color to have a recurring role on a national TV series.
  • 1972 Ms. Tyson was nominated for Best Actress by the Academy and the Golden Globe, for her role in Sounder. She won Best Actress from NSFC and NBR for that same role.
  • 1974 Ms. Tyson had her first title role as Miss Jane Pittman. She earned a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a miniseries or movie, and an Emmy Award for Actress of the Year in a special.

    • Ms. Tyson has been credited as an Actress 94 different times. She also was credited in two Soundtracks, appeared as herself 140 times, and was a Producer in a TV movie, A Trip to Bountiful.

    Ms. Tyson has won 3 Primetime Emmys, 4 Black Reels, a Screen Actors Guild, a Tony, and a Peabody Award. She was honorary Academy Award recipient. Ms. Tyson was also awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and the Kennedy Center Honors. Ms. Tyson was the first African American woman to recieve a honoray Oscar. Ms. Tyson has a total of 49 award wins and 52 nominations.

  • January 28, 2021 Cicely Tyson died of natural causes at the age of 96. She was a pioneer and a GIANT in the Film Industry.
